The Idea

It would be great if there was a way to share secrets between many parties, human or otherwise, without needing to pass around secret information like a key or password out of band. PGP can do this, but the keys necessary are large and using PGP is often viewed as cumbersome and complicated.

How About NaCl?

A relatively new cryptographic library called NaCl provides a public-key cryptography implementation and aim's to ensure it is "difficult to use incorrectly" in contrast to RSA which PGP generally leverages.

But MultiParty!

Unfortunately NaCl's simplicity comes at the cost of certain features; one of which is multi-party communication. NaCl excells at point to point communication with a single ciphertext. If multiple parties need a secret they must also share a key or have a distinct ciphertext created with their public key.

Enter MSG

The idea for MSG came out of various conversations with people at Bocoup and with Calvin Metcalf. MSG leverages NaCl's asymmetric crypto to secure a symmetric key used to encrypt the original message with AES-256-GCM. The ciphertext is a concatenation which can be broken appart and decrypted in pieces.

Shoe-Phone Secrets Umami Pact?

Pact is an application that utilizes the MSG library in a practical way and allows secure multiparty communication without requiring out of band secret sharing. Something neither AES-256-GCM or NaCl is capable of on their own.
